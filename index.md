<p>
  Hello! My name is Youngil (Steve) Ko. I am a M.S student at the <a href="https://kngpdl.kaist.ac.kr/">KAIST Non-equilibrium Gas and Plasma Dynamics Lab</a>, led by Professor Eunji Jun.
</p>

<p>
  My research is centered around exploring the intricacies of nonequilibrium gas and plasma dynamics. More specifically, I have a keen interest in developing models for gas-surface interactions, a crucial element in predicting surface properties within environments characterized by extreme flows.
</p>

<p>
  To investigate phenomena related to gas-surface interactions, I employ particle-based numerical methods like direct simulation Monte Carlo. Currently, my focus is on enhancing the finite-rate surface chemistry model for particle-based flow simulations. I aim to extend this work to continuum methods, such as Computational Fluid Dynamics, in order to expand the scope of my research to diverse flow conditions.
</p>

<p>
  <b> I am a Ph.D applicant for Fall 2025!</b>
</p>
