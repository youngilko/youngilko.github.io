<p>
Hello! My name is <b>Youngil Ko</b>. I am a first-year <b>Ph.D. student</b> working with Professor <a href="https://www.maedagroup.com/">Kazuki Maeda</a> in the Maeda Research Group at <b>Purdue University</b>’s <b>AAE</b>.
</p>

<p>
My primary interest is <b>reactive flows and combustion</b> in aerospace applications, which I study through multiscale modeling using molecular dynamics (<b>MD</b>), <b>DSMC</b>, and <b>CFD</b> on GPU-accelerated <b>HPC</b> platforms. I am also interested in techniques to extract and evaluate relevant flow physics from raw data.
</p>

<p>
I received my B.S. and M.S. from KAIST, where I worked with Professor <a href="https://kngpdl.kaist.ac.kr/">Eunji Jun</a> on rarefied gas dynamics, atmosphere-breathing electric propulsion (ABEP), and hypersonic aerothermodynamics. My M.S. thesis focused on analysis of molecular-scale surface catalytic effects.
</p>
